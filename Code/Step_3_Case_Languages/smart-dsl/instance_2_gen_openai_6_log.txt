### Key differences: Grammar 1 → Grammar 2

1. **`Modifier` renamed/replaced by `Validator`**
   - Grammar 1 rule: `Modifier: 'Modifier' name=ID ...`
   - Grammar 2 rule: `Validator: 'validator' name=ID ...`
   - Keyword casing changes too: **`Modifier`** (capital M) vs **`validator`** (lowercase).

2. **Contract body element list updated**
   - Grammar 1 allows inside `Contract`:
     - `attributes | events | methods | modifiers`
   - Grammar 2 allows:
     - `attributes | events | methods | validators | dataStores`
   - So: **`modifiers` removed**, **`validators` added**, **`dataStores` added**.

3. **Method references changed from modifiers to validators**
   - Grammar 1 `Method` block:
     - `modifiersKeyword='modifiers:' modifiers+=[Modifier] ...`
   - Grammar 2 `Method` block:
     - `modifiersKeyword='validators:' validators+=[Validator] ...`
   - So both the **keyword** and the **referenced type** changed.

4. **New `DataStore` concept added**
   - Grammar 2 introduces:
     ```xtext
     DataStore:
       'dataStore' name=ID 'fromType' fromType=DataType 'toType' toType=DataType;
     ```
   - This is completely new in Grammar 2 (no equivalent in Grammar 1).

5. **Everything else is effectively unchanged**
   - `Contract` header fields (`version`, optional `hasReceive`, optional `ownership`) are the same.
   - `Attribute`, `Event`, `Param`, `Output`, and `DataType` enum are unchanged (same tokens/structure).
